D.C. weather forecast: Finally above freezing, big temperature spike next week

January 26, 2013 - 10:30 PM
  • Email
  • Print
By Steve Rudin (Twitter @SteveRudinABC7)
Text size Decrease Increase

Overnight: Partly Cloudy

Lows: 15°-22° | Wind: Northwest 2-5 mph

Sunday: Partly to Mostly Cloudy

Highs: 32°-37° | Wind: West 5-10 mph

Monday: Freezing Rain to Rain Possible

Highs: 35°-40° | Wind: South 10-15 mph

Have you "liked" the new StormWatch7 Facebook page? Check it out here. Plus, be sure to follow ABC 7's Steve Rudin on Facebook and Twitter @SteveRudinABC7.

After a day with above freezing temperatures, big changes are on the way. A few clouds will remain overnight as surface high pressure builds. Lows will drop into the teens to lower 20s. Sunday will bring a bit more sunshine with additional cloud coverage by late afternoon. Highs should manage to rebound into the middle 30s.

A warm front will lift northward Monday. Ahead of the front, look for a chance of light freezing rain, quickly changing to all rain as warmer air settles across the mid-Atlantic. Highs will be around 40 degrees. A significant bump in temperatures will bring smiles to some (not to snow lovers) as highs jump well into the 50s to near 60 degrees. A cold front late Wednesday or early Thursday morning will drop highs for the rest of the week.

Stay with ABC 7 News, NewsChannel 8 and WJLA.com for the latest weather headlines.
